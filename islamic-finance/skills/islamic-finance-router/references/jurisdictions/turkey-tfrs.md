## GOVERNING FRAMEWORK
Turkey's "Participation Banks" (Katılım Bankaları) are the Turkish equivalent of Islamic banks.
They operate under the Banking Law No. 5411 and BDDK's specific regulations for participation banking.
TFRS is IFRS-equivalent; the Public Oversight, Accounting and Auditing Standards Authority
of Turkey (POA) issues standards.

Key institutions: Kuveyt Türk (majority owned by Kuwait Finance House),
Türkiye Finans (majority owned by Saudi National Bank), Albaraka Türk, Ziraat Katılım.

## TERMINOLOGY NOTE
Turkish participation banks use Turkish-language product names:
- Murabaha → "Taksitli Satış Finansmanı" (instalment sale finance)
- Ijarah → "Finansal Kiralama" (financial leasing) — but applied under AAOIFI-inspired principles
- DM → "Azalan Ortaklık" or "Ortak Girişim" (decreasing partnership)
- IAH → "Katılım Hesapları" (participation accounts)

## BDDK REGULATIONS
BDDK has issued specific guidance for participation banking operations.
Communique on Financial Statements for Banks (2012) governs reporting.
TFRS applies with BDDK adaptations. Non-interest income terminology required.
