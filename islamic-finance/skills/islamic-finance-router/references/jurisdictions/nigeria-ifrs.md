## GOVERNING FRAMEWORK
Nigeria applies IFRS as adopted by the Financial Reporting Council of Nigeria (FRCN).
The CBN's Non-Interest Banking Framework (2011) governs Islamic/non-interest banks.

Key institution: Jaiz Bank — Nigeria's first and largest fully-fledged non-interest bank.
Nigerian Sukuk Programme: Debt Management Office (DMO) ijarah sukuk for road infrastructure.
Islamic banking windows: TAJBank, Lotus Bank, and windows of conventional banks.

## CBN NON-INTEREST BANKING FRAMEWORK
- Governs all non-interest (Islamic) banking operations in Nigeria.
- Requires compliance with AAOIFI Shariah Standards for product structuring.
- Financial reporting: IFRS (same as conventional banks).
- CBN Prudential Guidelines apply to non-interest banks (same framework as conventional).

## NIGERIAN SOVEREIGN SUKUK
DMO programme: Multiple tranches issued since 2017. Ijarah structure.
Underlying assets: Federal road infrastructure assets.
Accounting treatment: As per IFRS 9 (issuer: failed derecognition, financial liability).
Investors: IFRS 9 amortised cost (HTC, SPPI pass for fixed distribution sukuk).

## SEC NIGERIA
Islamic capital market products regulated under Investments and Securities Act 2007.
SEC Guidelines for Non-Interest Capital Market Products govern sukuk issuance.
