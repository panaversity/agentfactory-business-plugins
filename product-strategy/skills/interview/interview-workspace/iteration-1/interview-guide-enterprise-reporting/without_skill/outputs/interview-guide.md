# Interview Guide: Custom Reporting Needs — Enterprise Customers

## Purpose
Understand why enterprise customers request custom reporting and what features would meet their needs.

## Target Audience
CFOs and Finance Directors at companies with 500+ employees

## Duration
60 minutes

## Part 1: Introduction (5 min)
- Thank the participant for their time
- Explain the purpose: we want to understand their reporting needs
- Get permission to record

## Part 2: Role and Context (10 min)
1. Tell me about your role as [CFO/Finance Director].
2. What does your reporting stack look like today?
3. How many people on your team interact with reports regularly?

## Part 3: Current Reporting Workflow (15 min)
4. Walk me through how you currently create and distribute reports.
5. What reporting tools do you use? (BI tools, Excel, our product, etc.)
6. How often do you generate reports?
7. What types of reports are most important to you?
8. How satisfied are you with your current reporting capabilities on a scale of 1-10?

## Part 4: Custom Reporting Needs (15 min)
9. You've requested custom reporting from us. Can you tell me what you were trying to accomplish?
10. What specific customizations would be most valuable?
11. Would you prefer self-service report building or having reports built for you?
12. How important is real-time data vs. periodic snapshots?
13. What would the ideal reporting dashboard look like for you?
14. Would you be willing to pay more for advanced reporting features?

## Part 5: Decision Making (10 min)
15. Who makes purchasing decisions for analytics and reporting tools?
16. What's your budget for reporting solutions?
17. What competing products have you evaluated?
18. What would make you switch to a different reporting solution?

## Part 6: Wrap Up (5 min)
19. Any other thoughts on reporting you'd like to share?
20. May we contact you for follow-up questions?

## Analysis Framework
After completing interviews, categorize findings into:
- Must-have features
- Nice-to-have features
- Unexpected insights
- Common pain points
