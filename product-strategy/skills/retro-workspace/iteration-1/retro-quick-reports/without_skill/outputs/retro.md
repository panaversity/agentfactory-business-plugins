# Quick Reports Feature Retrospective

**Feature**: Quick Reports
**Launch Date**: ~6 weeks ago
**Retro Date**: March 19, 2026

## What Went Well

- **Support ticket reduction**: Reports-related support tickets dropped by 60%, showing the feature successfully addressed a significant pain point.
- **NPS improvement**: NPS for the reporting area improved from 6.2 to 7.1, indicating users who interact with the feature are more satisfied.
- **Team delivered despite scope change**: Even with the PDF export addition mid-sprint, the team only slipped by 1 week.

## What Didn't Go Well

- **Adoption below target**: 34% adoption vs. 50% target means we're missing a large portion of the intended user base.
- **Scope creep**: PDF export was added mid-sprint, causing a 1-week delay. This suggests our scope management process needs tightening.
- **Late delivery**: The 1-week delay, while not severe, indicates planning gaps.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Target | Actual | Status |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Adoption | 50% | 34% | Below target |
| Support tickets | Decrease | -60% | Exceeded |
| NPS | Improve | 6.2 → 7.1 | Exceeded |

## Action Items

- Investigate why adoption is below target — is it awareness, UX, or something else?
- Implement a scope change process to prevent mid-sprint additions without proper evaluation
- Consider a feature promotion campaign to boost adoption

## Lessons Learned

- Strong technical execution can partially compensate for process issues, but it's not sustainable.
- The feature itself appears to solve the core problem well (based on support and NPS data), but getting users to it is a separate challenge.

## Next Steps

- Schedule user research sessions to understand the adoption gap
- Review scope change process with the team
- Set a 90-day check-in to re-evaluate adoption metrics
