# Jurisdiction-Specific Job Description Requirements

## United Kingdom

**Governing law**: Equality Act 2010

- **Protected characteristics**: age, disability, gender reassignment, marriage/civil partnership, pregnancy/maternity, race, religion/belief, sex, sexual orientation
- **Reasonable adjustments**: Must offer in every JD (legal requirement under Equality Act s.20)
- **Salary transparency**: Not yet legally mandated but considered best practice; EU Pay Transparency Directive (2023) will influence UK norms
- **Equal opportunities statement**: Expected by most candidates; standard wording available from EHRC
- **Right to work**: May state "must have right to work in UK" but cannot specify nationality

## United States

**Governing law**: Title VII (Civil Rights Act), ADA, ADEA, state laws

- **EEOC compliance**: Cannot require characteristics unrelated to job performance
- **ADA reasonable accommodation**: Must include willingness to provide reasonable accommodation for qualified individuals with disabilities
- **Salary transparency**: Varies by state — mandatory in CO, CA, NY, WA, CT (as of 2024); check state law before publishing
- **Ban-the-box**: Many states/cities prohibit asking about criminal history in job postings
- **BFOQ (Bona Fide Occupational Qualification)**: Only lawful exception to protected class requirements; rare and narrowly applied

## Pakistan

**Governing law**: No single comprehensive anti-discrimination employment statute

- **Salary disclosure**: Not legally required; common practice is to list range or "market competitive"
- **Gender**: Constitutional equality provisions (Article 25); Punjab Fair Representation of Women Act 2014 applies to public sector
- **Disability**: Disabled Persons Employment and Rehabilitation Ordinance 1981 — 2% quota for establishments with 100+ employees
- **Age limits**: Common in postings but increasingly discouraged by HR best practice
- **Language**: Postings typically in English for professional roles; Urdu for operational roles

## United Arab Emirates

**Governing law**: Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021 (Labour Law), Ministerial Resolution No. 44 of 2022

- **Nationality preferences**: Prohibited under Article 4 of the Labour Law (non-discrimination clause)
- **Emiratisation**: Private sector companies with 50+ employees must meet Emiratisation quotas (increasing annually); JDs for Emiratisation-eligible roles should note this
- **Gender**: Equal pay for equal work mandated (Article 4)
- **Salary**: Must be stated in employment contract; JD best practice is to include range
- **Reasonable adjustments**: Ministerial Resolution supports employment of persons with disabilities; accommodation expectations should be stated

## General Guidance

When jurisdiction is not specified in hr.local.md:

1. Apply UK Equality Act principles as a baseline (broadest protected characteristics list)
2. Always include reasonable adjustments offer
3. Always include equal opportunities statement
4. Always include salary range
5. Flag jurisdiction-specific items for HR review before publishing
