# Jurisdiction-Specific Offboarding Requirements

## United Kingdom

**Statutory requirements**:

- **P45**: Employer must issue a P45 (Details of employee leaving work) to the departing employee on or before their last day. Contains tax code, total pay and tax for the year.
- **Statutory notice**: Minimum 1 week after 1 month of service; 1 week per year of service after 2 years (capped at 12 weeks). Contractual notice may be longer.
- **Garden leave**: Employer may place employee on garden leave during notice period (employee remains employed but does not attend work). Must be provided for in the employment contract.
- **Accrued holiday**: Untaken statutory holiday (5.6 weeks) must be paid in lieu on departure. Check contract for additional entitlement.
- **Restrictive covenants**: Non-compete, non-solicitation clauses activate on departure — remind the employee and confirm in writing.
- **Pension**: Auto-enrolment cessation; final contribution processing.
- **Reference**: No obligation to provide (except regulated sectors); if provided, must be true, accurate, and fair.

**Data**: HMRC PAYE guidance (gov.uk); ACAS leaving your job guide (acas.org.uk)

## United States

**No single federal offboarding statute** — requirements vary by state:

- **COBRA**: Employer with 20+ employees must offer continuation of health insurance for up to 18 months. Notice must be provided within 14 days of qualifying event.
- **WARN Act**: 60 days' advance notice required for plant closings or mass layoffs (100+ employees affected). State "mini-WARN" laws may have lower thresholds.
- **Final pay**: State-specific deadlines — California requires final pay on the last day (or within 72 hours if employee quits without notice). New York allows regular next payday. Always check state law.
- **PTO payout**: State-dependent — some states require payout of accrued unused PTO; others follow employer policy.
- **401(k)**: Provide information on rollover options; process final contributions.
- **Non-compete**: Enforceability varies dramatically by state (unenforceable in California; enforceable with limitations in most other states). FTC proposed ban (2024) — check current status.

**Data**: DOL (dol.gov); state labour department websites

## Pakistan

**Governing law**: Employment-specific statutes vary by province (e.g., Sindh Shops Act, Punjab Shops Act, Industrial Relations Act)

- **EOBI (Employees' Old-Age Benefits Institution)**: Final contribution must be processed. Employee receives EOBI card/record for future claims.
- **Gratuity**: Employees with 1+ year of continuous service are entitled to gratuity (typically 30 days' last drawn salary per year of service). Calculate and include in final settlement.
- **Notice period**: Per employment contract or applicable provincial law (typically 1 month for permanent employees).
- **Experience certificate**: Customary and expected — confirms employment dates, role, and basic performance.
- **Final settlement**: Last salary + accrued leave encashment + gratuity + any other contractual entitlements. Timeline: typically within 30 days of last working day.
- **Provident fund**: If applicable, process final provident fund withdrawal or transfer documentation.

**Data**: EOBI (eobi.gov.pk); provincial labour department regulations

## United Arab Emirates

**Governing law**: Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021

- **End-of-service gratuity**: Calculated based on length of service:
  - 1-5 years: 21 days' basic salary per year
  - 5+ years: 30 days' basic salary per year (for years beyond 5)
  - Total gratuity cannot exceed 2 years' salary
  - Applies to employees with 1+ year of continuous service
- **Visa cancellation**: Employer must cancel the employee's work visa within 14 days of contract termination. Employee has 30-day grace period to find new employment, transfer visa, or exit the country.
- **Final settlement**: Must be completed within 14 days of last working day (Article 53)
- **End-of-service certificate**: Mandatory under Article 13 — must include dates, title, last salary. Cannot include negative content that harms employment prospects.
- **Notice period**: As per contract; statutory minimum varies by contract type (30-90 days under the new Labour Law)
- **Non-compete**: Enforceable if reasonable in scope, geography, and duration (max 2 years)

**Data**: MOHRE (mohre.gov.ae); UAE Labour Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 33 of 2021)

## Offboarding Checklist — Jurisdiction-Agnostic Items

These apply regardless of jurisdiction:

1. System access removal (document every system)
2. Device return and data wipe
3. Email forwarding / auto-reply setup
4. Handover plan completion confirmation
5. Exit interview (Week 3 of notice, conducted by HRBP)
6. Final pay calculation (salary + accrued leave + any entitlements)
7. Benefits cessation processing
8. Rehire eligibility documented
9. Reference policy communicated to departing employee
