SCOPE DETERMINATION: REQUEST OUTSIDE /match CAPABILITY
================================================================

**User request:** "We're restructuring the engineering department from functional teams to product-aligned squads. Can you help me figure out which teams to create and who should lead each one?"

**Assessment:** This request contains two distinct components:

1. **"Which teams to create"** — This is an **org design / restructuring** task. It involves defining team structures, reporting lines, product alignment, and operating model decisions. This falls outside the scope of /match and should be handled by **/org-planning** (organizational design and restructuring).

2. **"Who should lead each one"** — Once specific squad lead roles are defined with clear requirements and internal candidates are identified, /match *can* assess candidate readiness for those roles. However, /match cannot be activated until:
   - The new squad structure has been decided (org-planning output)
   - Specific squad lead role requirements are defined (critical, important, nice-to-have competencies)
   - Internal candidates have been identified for each squad lead role (at least 2 per role)

**The /match skill description explicitly states:**
> NOT for: org restructuring (use org-planning)

================================================================
WHAT /match NEEDS TO PROCEED (once org design is complete)
================================================================

For each squad lead role, /match requires:

**Required information (must be provided):**
- What role or level are candidates being assessed for?
  → e.g., "Squad Lead — Payments Product Squad (Senior Engineering Manager level)"
- Who are the candidates? (at least 2 names with current roles and tenure)
  → e.g., "Sarah Chen, Backend Team Lead, 3 years; Marcus Hall, Platform Engineer, 5 years"
- Is this a current vacancy, succession planning, or development mapping?
  → In a restructuring context, likely "current vacancy" for each new squad lead role

**Optional but valuable:**
- Timeline urgency (when does the new structure go live?)
- Specific competencies to weight heavily (e.g., cross-functional collaboration, product thinking)
- Defined readiness criteria for the squad lead level

================================================================
RECOMMENDED WORKFLOW
================================================================

1. **FIRST:** Use /org-planning to design the squad structure
   - Define product-aligned squads (number, scope, mission)
   - Define each squad lead role's requirements
   - Map current functional team members to proposed squads

2. **THEN:** Use /match for each squad lead role
   - Assess 2+ internal candidates per squad lead position
   - Six-dimension assessment (critical skills, experience, trajectory, readiness indicators, development areas, motivation)
   - Readiness classification (Ready Now / Ready in 6 months / Ready in 12 months / Developing / Not a Fit)
   - Development plans for candidates not yet ready

3. **FINALLY:** Use /match output to inform leadership decisions
   - Priority order of candidates per role
   - Retention risk for high-potential candidates not selected
   - Succession conversation guidance

================================================================
UNABLE TO PRODUCE TALENT ASSESSMENT
================================================================

A talent assessment cannot be generated because:

- [ ] No specific target role defined (squad lead roles not yet designed)
- [ ] No candidates identified (names, current roles, tenure not provided)
- [ ] No role requirements specified (critical, important, nice-to-have competencies)
- [ ] Assessment context unclear without org design decisions made first

**This is not a refusal — it is a sequencing recommendation.** /match is the right tool for step 2 of this process, but step 1 (org design) must come first.
