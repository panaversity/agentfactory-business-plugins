## Why These Are Declined

### 1. Termination Letter -- Cannot Generate

**This skill will NEVER generate termination or redundancy letters.**
These documents require:

- A **qualified HR professional** to draft (employment law varies by jurisdiction, and errors create significant legal exposure)
- **Legal review** before issuance (wrongful termination claims are among the most common and costly employment disputes)
- Alignment with your organisation's **disciplinary and capability procedures** (has the employee been through a documented performance improvement process?)

**What you should do instead:**

- Contact your HR Business Partner or employment law advisor
- Ensure you have documented evidence of the performance management process (warnings, PIP, review meetings)
- Confirm the termination is procedurally compliant with your jurisdiction's employment law (e.g., ACAS Code of Practice in the UK, EEOC guidelines in the US)

### 2. Redundancy Process -- Clarification Needed

You mentioned both **termination for poor performance** and **redundancy**. These are legally distinct processes:

| | Performance Termination | Redundancy |
|---|---|---|
| **Reason** | Employee's capability/conduct | Role no longer exists |
| **Legal basis** | Capability/conduct dismissal | Economic/organisational restructuring |
| **Process** | PIP → warnings → termination | Consultation → selection → notice |
| **Risk if confused** | Unfair dismissal claim | Unfair dismissal + failure to consult |

**Conflating these is a significant legal risk.** If the role still exists and you are replacing the employee, this is a performance termination, not a redundancy. Calling it redundancy exposes the organisation to claims of sham redundancy.

## What This Skill CAN Help With

Once the termination has been properly executed by HR and legal, the /offboard skill can help you with the **post-termination offboarding process**:

- **Phase 1**: Immediate actions (access removal, payroll notification, HRIS updates)
- **Phase 2**: Knowledge handover planning (capturing institutional knowledge before departure)
- **Phase 3**: Exit interview (if appropriate and the employee is willing)
- **Phase 4**: Last day checklist (systems access removal, device return, final documentation)
