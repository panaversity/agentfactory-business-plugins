# Senior Software Engineer — Platform Team

London (Hybrid — 3 days on-site) | £85,000 – £110,000

## About the Role

We're looking for a Senior Software Engineer to join our Platform team — a group of 6 engineers building the internal developer tools and CI/CD infrastructure that the rest of engineering depends on. You'll shape how our engineers ship software every day, and you'll mentor two mid-level engineers as they grow.

This role matters because our engineering organisation is scaling, and the quality of our internal tooling directly determines how fast and safely every team can deliver.

## The Work

- **Own and evolve our CI/CD pipelines** — design, build, and operate the continuous integration and delivery infrastructure that all product teams rely on to ship safely and frequently.
- **Build internal developer tools** — create CLIs, dashboards, and automation that remove friction from the development workflow, from local setup to production deployment.
- **Drive platform reliability and observability** — instrument systems so teams can understand performance, diagnose failures, and trust the platform under load.
- **Mentor two mid-level engineers** — pair regularly, review their designs, and help them grow into independent technical decision-makers.
- **Shape technical direction** — you will have the opportunity to define platform standards and influence the engineering culture around developer experience across the organisation.

## What We're Looking For

### Essential

- **Strong software engineering fundamentals** — you can design, build, and operate production systems. You reason well about trade-offs in distributed systems, testing strategies, and deployment patterns.
- **Proficiency with CI/CD systems and infrastructure-as-code** — hands-on ability with tools such as GitHub Actions, GitLab CI, Jenkins, Terraform, or equivalent. We care about depth of understanding, not years on a specific tool.
- **Experience building tools for other engineers** — you've built internal platforms, developer tooling, or infrastructure that other teams depended on, and you've iterated based on their feedback.
- **Mentoring ability** — you've helped less experienced engineers grow, whether through pairing, code review, or design coaching. This is a core part of the role, not an afterthought.

### Beneficial (not required)

- Familiarity with container orchestration (Kubernetes, ECS, or similar)
- Experience with observability tooling (Datadog, Grafana, Prometheus, OpenTelemetry)
- Contributions to open-source developer tools
- Experience working in a platform or infrastructure team at a scaling organisation

### Character / Ways of Working

- **User-minded** — you treat other engineers as your customers and care about the developer experience of the tools you build.
- **Clear communicator** — you explain technical decisions in plain language and write documentation that people actually use.

## What We Offer

- **Salary:** £85,000 – £110,000 depending on experience
- **Location:** London, hybrid working — 3 days per week on-site, 2 days remote
- **Team:** A collaborative Platform team of 6 engineers with a strong focus on developer experience
- **Growth:** Direct mentoring responsibility, influence over platform technical direction, and support for conference attendance and professional development
