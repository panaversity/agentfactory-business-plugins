## IFRS 9 ECL — UAE
IFRS 9 as issued by IASB applies for all CBUAE-regulated banks.
CBUAE guidance: CBUAE Circular on IFRS 9 implementation (2018) and subsequent guidance.
No material carve-outs from IASB IFRS 9 for impairment.

## CAPITAL — CBUAE
Minimum CET1: 7.0% (higher than Basel 4.5% — CBUAE conservative approach)
Minimum T1: 8.5%
Minimum TC: 10.5%
Capital Conservation Buffer: 2.5%
D-SIB surcharge: Up to 2.0% for CBUAE-designated D-SIBs (Emirates NBD, ENBD, FAB, etc.)

Effective CET1 minimum (illustrative for D-SIB): 7.0% + 2.5% + 1.5% = 11.0%

CBUAE Leverage Ratio: 3.0% minimum

CBUAE Basel IV: Consultation ongoing. CBUAE is a BCBS observer and generally follows
  Basel standards closely. Check CBUAE website for current implementation status.

## LIQUIDITY — CBUAE
LCR: 100% minimum (monthly, moving to more frequent reporting)
UAE Government securities qualify as Level 1 HQLA.
NSFR: 100% minimum (implemented 2019).

## AML — UAE
Federal Decree Law No. 20 of 2018: primary AML/CFT legislation
Cabinet Decision No. 10 of 2019: implementing regulation
AML/CFT Supervision: CBUAE for banks; Central Bank for exchange houses; DFSA for DIFC

UAE FIU: goAML platform for STR/SAR filing
STR filing standard: "Suspicion or reasonable grounds to suspect" proceeds of crime or TF
No minimum amount for STR.

UAE is a FATF member. UAE exited FATF grey list in 2024 after implementing required
  action plan. Continued enhanced scrutiny by international correspondents remains.

CBUAE AML Guidelines for Licensed Financial Institutions (LFIs): comprehensive CDD/EDD
  framework aligned with FATF recommendations.
Customer risk rating: CBUAE requires risk-based approach with documented methodology.

DIFC / ADGM:
  DFSA AML Module: applies to DIFC-registered banks (based on FATF standards).
  FSRA AML Rulebook: applies to ADGM-registered entities.
  Both are internationally aligned but maintain separate regulatory frameworks.
