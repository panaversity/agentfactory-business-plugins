## IFRS 9 ECL — SINGAPORE (FRS 109)
FRS 109 is adopted IFRS 9 — all IFRS 9 ECL rules apply.
MAS Notice 612 (Credit Files, Grading and Provisioning): MAS prescribes minimum
  provisioning for non-performing assets, which may be more conservative than IFRS 9 ECL
  output for some exposures. Apply the HIGHER of FRS 109 ECL and MAS Notice 612 minimum.
MAS NPL definition: broadly consistent with IFRS 9 Stage 3; MAS uses "non-performing"
  classification at 90 days past due.

## CAPITAL — MAS NOTICE 637 (BASEL III)
Minimum CET1: 6.5% (higher than Basel 4.5% — MAS imposes domestic surcharge)
Minimum T1: 8.0% (higher than Basel 6.0%)
Minimum TC: 10.0% (higher than Basel 8.0%)
Capital Conservation Buffer: 2.5% (same as Basel)
D-SIB surcharge: 2.0% for DBS, OCBC, UOB (Singapore-incorporated D-SIBs)

Effective CET1 minimum (D-SIBs): 6.5% + 2.5% + 2.0% = 11.0%
Singapore has maintained higher minimum ratios than Basel since Basel III implementation.

MAS Notice 637 also implements:
  Leverage ratio: 3.0% minimum (Basel standard)
  LCR: 100% minimum (daily reporting)
  NSFR: 100% minimum

MAS Basel IV implementation: MAS has consulted on Basel IV implementation — check MAS website for
  current status of output floor and revised SA implementation.

## AML — MAS NOTICES
MAS Notice 626 (Banks): Primary AML/CFT notice for banks
MAS Notice 1014 (Finance Companies), Notice 824 (Merchant Banks) — similar requirements

STR (Suspicious Transaction Report): File with Commercial Affairs Department (CAD) / STRO
  (Suspicious Transaction Reporting Office) — not MAS directly.
STRO portal: goAML (same as UAE FMU)
Standard: "Knows or has reason to suspect" proceeds of criminal conduct.
No minimum amount for STR filing.

MAS Guidance on AML/CFT Controls: MAS has issued extensive guidance on risk-based
  CDD, correspondent banking, digital payment token services.

Singapore FATF status: FATF member; generally rated "compliant" or "largely compliant"
  in mutual evaluations. Singapore is a major international financial centre with
  correspondingly high AML/CFT obligations.
