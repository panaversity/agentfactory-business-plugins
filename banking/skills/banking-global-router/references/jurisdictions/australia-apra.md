## IFRS 9 ECL — AUSTRALIA (AASB 9)
AASB 9 is adopted IFRS 9 — all IFRS 9 ECL rules apply without material modification.
APRA Prudential Standard APS 220 (Credit Quality): governs classification of impaired assets.
APRA definition of "impaired" broadly consistent with IFRS 9 Stage 3.
APRA reports data on NPLs and specific provisions from ADI (Authorised Deposit-taking Institution) returns.

## CAPITAL — APRA APS STANDARDS
APRA has implemented Basel III with "unquestionably strong" benchmarks above Basel minimums.

Minimum CET1: 4.5% (APRA APS 110)
Capital Conservation Buffer: 2.5%
Domestic Systemically Important Bank (D-SIB) surcharge: 1.0% (Big 4 + Macquarie)
Countercyclical Buffer: 0%–2.5% (set by APRA; check current rate at apra.gov.au)

APRA "unquestionably strong" target: CET1 ≥ 10.5% for major banks (indicative)
  (based on APRA's July 2017 benchmark — banks have generally operated well above this)

APRA Leverage Ratio: 3.5% (higher than Basel 3.0% minimum; applies to domestically systemically important banks)

APRA Basel IV implementation: APRA implementing Basel IV via APS 112/113 updates.
  Output floor: being implemented through APRA's revised standardised approach constraints.

APRA stress test: APRA conducts periodic stress tests (Industry-Wide Stress Testing, IWST).
  APRA stress test scenarios: severe recession, housing price correction, global financial shock.

## LIQUIDITY — APRA
LCR: APRA APS 210. 100% minimum. Daily reporting for major banks.
  Australian HQLA: Commonwealth Government Securities (CGS) qualify as Level 1.
  Committed Liquidity Facility (CLF): APRA allows Australian banks to use RBA CLF
  to meet LCR given limited CGS supply — check if CLF still available (APRA reviewing post-2023)
NSFR: APRA APS 210 (updated). 100% minimum.

## AML — AUSTRAC / AML-CTF ACT 2006
AUSTRAC is Australia's financial intelligence unit and AML/CTF regulator.
AML/CTF Act 2006: primary legislation (modelled on FATF 40 Recommendations)

Key obligations:
  SMR (Suspicious Matter Report): file when transaction involves proceeds of crime or
    financing of terrorism. No dollar threshold — all suspicious activity.
  TTR (Threshold Transaction Report): file for all cash transactions ≥ AUD 10,000.
  IFTIs (International Funds Transfer Instructions): report all international wire transfers
    (sent and received) regardless of amount. This is unique to Australia — very broad scope.

Filing: AUSTRAC Online portal
Tipping-off: criminal offence under AML/CTF Act s.123
