## Pre-Plan Grilling (Brooks Principle)

Before I build the calendar, I need to sharpen the inputs. You said "AP Managers at mid-market manufacturers" — that's a description, not a buying trigger. Let me fill in what I'm assuming and flag where I need you:

**What I'm assuming (correct me):**

1. **Product category:** AP automation software (invoice processing, approval workflows, payment execution)
2. **Mid-market manufacturer:** $50M–$500M revenue, 5–50 person finance team
3. **ACV range:** $15K–$40K/year (typical for this segment)
4. **Sales cycle:** 30–60 days with a champion + finance leader sign-off
5. **Current alternative:** Manual AP in ERP (SAP, NetSuite, Sage) + spreadsheets + email approvals

**What I need from you:**

- **Buying trigger:** What event makes an AP Manager ready to buy *right now*? Examples: new ERP migration, audit finding, controller mandate to cut headcount, supplier threatening to cut terms. Which one(s) have you seen?
- **Deal size:** What are you charging (or planning to charge)?
- **Product readiness:** Is the product live and usable today, or is this pre-product?
- **Do you have any existing relationships** in manufacturing? Former employers, advisors, warm intros?
- **Which founder does sales?** Is either of you from AP/finance or from manufacturing?

These aren't optional questions — the difference between a calendar that works and one that doesn't is whether the trigger, deal size, and founder network are real. A 2-person, $0-budget plan lives and dies on founder-led outreach precision.

Give me these and I'll build the week-by-week calendar with specific daily activities, decision gates, and the exact point where you pivot if something isn't working.
