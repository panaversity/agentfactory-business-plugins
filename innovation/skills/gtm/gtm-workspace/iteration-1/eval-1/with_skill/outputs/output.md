IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE (ICP)
Venture: AI-Powered AP Automation | Version: 1 | Based on: 10 discovery interviews + 3 pilot customers
================================================================

PRIMARY ICP:

  Company:
    Sector:       Manufacturing (discrete or process — not construction, not services)
    Size:         100–300 employees; $20M–$100M revenue
    Geography:    US / Canada (English-language invoices; USD/CAD transactions)
    Technology:   ERP in place (NetSuite, Sage, or mid-tier ERP) but AP workflow
                  is still spreadsheet/email-based; no existing AP automation tool
    Structure:    AP team of 1–4 people; no dedicated IT department or IT is
                  stretched thin (1–2 people supporting the whole company)

  Person:
    Role:         Accounts Payable Manager (or AP Lead / AP Supervisor —
                  NOT the CFO, NOT a staff AP clerk)
    Seniority:    Mid-level; manages 1–3 AP clerks; reports to Controller or
                  VP Finance; has influence on tool selection but needs
                  Controller sign-off for budget
    Cares about:  1. Reducing invoice processing time (measured in days, not hours)
                  2. Eliminating three-way matching errors that cause supplier disputes
                  3. Passing audits without scrambling — clean documentation trail
    How they buy: AP Manager identifies need → builds informal business case →
                  Controller approves budget (typically <$50K doesn't need CFO) →
                  30–60 day evaluation with a pilot on real invoices → decision

  Buying Trigger:
    The company has recently experienced ONE OR MORE of:
    - A failed or painful audit due to AP documentation gaps
    - A key AP clerk quit, exposing how manual and person-dependent the process is
    - Supplier disputes or duplicate payments that hit the P&L visibly
    - A new Controller/CFO arrived and is asking "why is this still manual?"

    Why trigger matters: Manufacturing AP managers have tolerated manual
    three-way matching for years. Without a forcing event, they will keep
    tolerating it. Prospects without a trigger browse but don't buy.

  Signals (how to identify ICP prospects):
    - Job postings for AP clerks at manufacturing companies in size range
      (signals understaffing or turnover — the trigger)
    - LinkedIn activity: AP Managers posting about "invoice backlog,"
      "month-end close," or "AP automation" in manufacturing groups
    - Companies that recently switched ERPs (implementation pain reopens
      tool evaluation for adjacent processes)
    - Attending manufacturing finance conferences (MFCA, IMA chapters)
    - Active in AP/finance communities on LinkedIn or Reddit r/Accounting

  NOT a fit (explicitly exclude):
    - Companies with <100 employees: AP volume too low to justify; long
      sales cycle for small deal size; high churn risk
    - Companies with >500 employees: already evaluating Coupa/SAP Ariba/
      Tipalti; your product isn't enterprise-ready yet; 6–12 month sales cycles
    - Services companies (consulting, agencies): low invoice volume;
      no three-way matching (no PO/receipt); different pain entirely
    - Companies already using Tipalti, Bill.com, or Coupa: switching cost
      too high; you're not displacing incumbents at this stage
    - Companies without an ERP: integration is your value prop; no ERP
      means a different (harder) problem
    - AP Clerks as buyers: they feel the pain but can't authorize budget

SECONDARY ICP:

  Company:
    Sector:       Wholesale distribution (similar PO → receipt → invoice flow)
    Size:         100–300 employees; $15M–$80M revenue
    Geography:    US / Canada
    Technology:   Same ERP profile as primary; high invoice volume per supplier
    Structure:    Small AP team; similar to manufacturing profile

  Person:
    Role:         AP Manager or Office Manager (in smaller distributors,
                  the Office Manager owns AP)
    Buying Trigger: Same triggers — audit pain, staff turnover, duplicate payments

  NOTE: Secondary ICP is based on pattern similarity, NOT validated.
  Requires 3–5 discovery interviews with wholesale distributors to confirm.

================================================================

ICP CONFIDENCE: MEDIUM
Based on 3 paying pilots (all manufacturing) and 10 interviews.
