## AP Team Customer Discovery Interview Guide

### Pre-Interview Setup
- **Target:** People who work in or manage AP functions (AP clerks, AP managers, Controllers, VP Finance)
- **Goal:** Understand their world, not pitch your idea
- **Duration:** 30-40 minutes
- **Recording:** Ask permission; take notes regardless

---

### Opening (2 min)

> "Thanks for making time. I'm researching how AP teams work day-to-day — what's hard, what's changed recently, what tools help or don't. I'm not selling anything today. There are no right or wrong answers. I just want to learn from your experience."

---

### Section 1: Context & Role (5 min)

1. What's your role, and how long have you been in AP?
2. How big is your AP team? How many invoices do you process per month (roughly)?
3. Walk me through a typical day — what takes up most of your time?

---

### Section 2: Current Process (10 min)

4. Can you walk me through what happens from when an invoice arrives to when it gets paid?
5. Where do invoices come from? (Email, portal, mail, EDI, etc.)
6. What systems do you use? (ERP, AP automation, spreadsheets, email?)
7. How do approvals work? What happens when an approver is slow or unavailable?
8. How do you handle exceptions — duplicate invoices, missing POs, pricing mismatches?

---

### Section 3: Pain & Frustration (10 min)

*This is the most important section. Go deep. Follow the energy.*

9. What's the most frustrating part of your job right now?
10. When was the last time something went really wrong in AP? What happened?
11. What causes the most back-and-forth or rework?
12. Are there tasks you do that feel like they shouldn't require a human?
13. What keeps you from closing the books on time at month-end?
14. Have you ever had issues with duplicate payments, missed discounts, or late payment penalties? How often?

---

### Section 4: Prior Attempts to Solve (5 min)

*Critical — tells you what they've already tried and why it failed.*

15. Have you tried any tools or process changes to fix these problems? What happened?
16. If you evaluated solutions and didn't buy — what stopped you?
17. What would a solution need to do for you to champion it internally?

---

### Section 5: Impact & Priorities (5 min)

18. If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing in AP, what would it be?
19. How does AP performance get measured? What metrics does your boss care about?
20. Is AP getting more attention from leadership lately? Why or why not?

---

### Closing (3 min)

21. Is there anything I should have asked but didn't?
22. Who else should I talk to — on your team or at other companies?
23. Would you be open to a follow-up conversation in a few weeks?

---

### Rules for the Interview

1. **Listen 80%, talk 20%.** Your job is to extract, not explain.
2. **Never pitch.** If they ask what you're building, say "still figuring it out — that's why I'm talking to people like you."
3. **Follow the emotion.** When they get animated or frustrated, ask "tell me more about that."
4. **Get specific.** "That's frustrating" → "Can you tell me about the last time that happened?"
5. **Avoid leading questions.** Not "Would it help if X automated Y?" — instead "How do you handle Y today?"
