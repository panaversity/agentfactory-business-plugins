## GOVERNING FRAMEWORK

This overlay provides awareness for automated systems generating outreach
content targeting GCC-based prospects. This is NOT legal advice. Consult
qualified legal counsel for compliance requirements.

### UAE -- TRA Anti-Spam Regulations

Federal Law No. 5 of 2012 (Combating Cybercrimes):

- Article 17: sending unsolicited electronic messages (spam) is
  prohibited; penalties include fines of AED 100,000 to AED 500,000
  and/or imprisonment
- Applies to email, SMS, and electronic messaging platforms
- Legitimate B2B communications with opt-out are generally
  distinguished from prohibited spam

Telecommunications and Digital Government Regulatory Authority (TDRA):

- Unsolicited bulk SMS: requires registered short code and operator
  agreement
- Commercial messaging: must include sender identification and
  opt-out mechanism
- DND lists: maintained by mobile operators; must check before
  SMS outreach

UAE Data Protection (Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 -- PDPL):

- Processing personal data requires lawful basis (consent or
  legitimate interest)
- Data subjects have rights: access, correction, erasure, objection
- Cross-border transfers: permitted to adequate jurisdictions or
  with consent
- Fines: up to AED 20 million for violations

DIFC Data Protection Law 2020:

- GDPR-aligned; stricter than mainland UAE
- Applies to processing data of individuals in DIFC
- Commissioner of Data Protection oversees compliance

ADGM Data Protection Regulations 2021:

- GDPR-aligned; applies within ADGM jurisdiction
- Applies to processing data of individuals in ADGM

ESCALATION TRIGGER: Outreach campaign targeting UAE free zone
entities (DIFC/ADGM) -> apply GDPR-equivalent protections, not
just mainland UAE rules

### Saudi Arabia -- CITC Regulations

Communications, Space and Technology Commission (CST, formerly CITC):

- Anti-spam regulations: bulk unsolicited commercial messages
  prohibited without prior consent
- SMS marketing: requires CST-approved short code and sender
  registration
- Commercial email: must include sender identification, opt-out
  mechanism, and physical address
- Penalties for violations: fines and potential service suspension

Saudi Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL, effective 2023):

- Processing personal data requires explicit consent or legitimate
  interest
- Data subjects have rights: access, correction, deletion
- Cross-border transfers: restricted to approved jurisdictions
  or with consent and adequate safeguards
- Data localisation: certain categories of personal data must
  remain in Saudi Arabia -- verify with counsel
- National Data Management Office (NDMO) oversees compliance

ESCALATION TRIGGER: Data enrichment of Saudi prospects using
non-Saudi data sources -> verify PDPL compliance and data
localisation requirements

### Bahrain -- Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL)

Law No. 30 of 2018:

- Comprehensive data protection law overseen by the Personal Data
  Protection Authority
- Processing requires consent or legitimate interest
- Marketing communications: consent required from individuals
- B2B outreach to business contacts at their professional capacity:
  legitimate interest may apply
- Cross-border transfers: permitted with adequate safeguards

### Cultural and Religious Considerations (All GCC)

Ramadan:

- Reduced working hours (typically 6 hours/day) across all GCC states
- Adjust outreach volume and timing during Ramadan
- Avoid scheduling calls during Iftar time (sunset)
- Sensitivity in messaging: avoid food/drink imagery, celebratory
  tone is appropriate for Ramadan greetings
- Business decisions often deferred until after Eid al-Fitr

Friday:

- Friday is the weekend day (or part of weekend) in GCC countries
- Weekend: Friday-Saturday in UAE, Saudi Arabia, Bahrain, Qatar
- Weekend: Friday-Saturday in Kuwait, Oman
- Do not schedule outreach for Fridays
- Thursday afternoons: reduced productivity as weekend approaches

National and religious holidays:

- Eid al-Fitr: 3-5 days (variable; based on moon sighting)
- Eid al-Adha: 3-5 days (variable; based on moon sighting)
- Saudi National Day (September 23)
- UAE National Day (December 2)
- Bahrain National Day (December 16-17)
- Pause all outreach during major holidays

Business culture:

- Relationship-driven: personal introductions and referrals
  carry significant weight
- Hierarchy matters: approach at the appropriate seniority level
- Decision-making: often centralised; identify the decision-maker
  early
- Arabic language: initial outreach in English is standard for
  international business; Arabic shows respect and investment
- Government and semi-government entities: formal communication
  channels preferred; LinkedIn outreach may be less effective

### LinkedIn and Digital Outreach in GCC

- LinkedIn adoption is high in UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Bahrain
  for professional B2B communications
- WhatsApp: widely used for business communications; acceptable
  for B2B contact after initial introduction
- Email: standard for formal B2B communications
- Phone: acceptable after initial contact established;
  cold calling is less common in GCC business culture

## KEY GCC-SPECIFIC OUTREACH TRIGGERS

- UAE anti-spam (Federal Law No. 5/2012): fines up to AED 500,000
  for unsolicited bulk messages; opt-out required in all commercial
  communications
- UAE PDPL: data protection rights apply; cross-border transfer
  restrictions
- DIFC/ADGM: GDPR-equivalent protections apply within free zones
- Saudi PDPL: explicit consent or legitimate interest required;
  data localisation for certain data categories
- Saudi CST: SMS marketing requires registered short code
- Bahrain PDPL: comprehensive data protection; marketing consent
  required from individuals
- Ramadan sensitivity: adjust timing, volume, and tone during
  holy month
- Weekend: Friday-Saturday across all GCC states; no Friday outreach
- Referrals: relationship introductions carry significant weight
  in GCC business culture
- Language: English standard for international B2B; Arabic for
  local/government entities
