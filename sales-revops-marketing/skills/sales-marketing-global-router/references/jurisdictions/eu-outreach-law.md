## GOVERNING FRAMEWORK

This overlay provides awareness for automated systems generating outreach
content targeting EU-based prospects. This is NOT legal advice. Consult
qualified legal counsel for compliance requirements.

### GDPR (Regulation 2016/679) -- Outreach Implications

The GDPR applies to processing personal data of individuals in the EU,
regardless of where the sender is located.

Key requirements for outreach systems:

Lawful basis for processing (Art. 6):

- Legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f)): primary basis for B2B outreach
  Must pass the three-part legitimate interest assessment (LIA):
  1. Purpose test: is there a legitimate business interest?
  2. Necessity test: is the processing necessary for that purpose?
  3. Balancing test: does the individual's interest override yours?
- Consent (Art. 6(1)(a)): required for B2C email marketing in most
  EU member states; strongest legal basis but harder to obtain

B2B vs B2C distinction:

- B2B: legitimate interest is generally accepted for initial outreach
  to business contacts at their work email for relevant business purposes
- B2C: opt-in consent typically required before any marketing contact
- Mixed: individual sole traders or partnerships may be treated as B2C
  in some member states -- verify with counsel

Right to object (Art. 21):

- Data subjects can object to processing based on legitimate interest
  at ANY time -- and objection must be honoured immediately
- Objection = permanent opt-out from that processing purpose
- Cannot require a reason for objection

Data minimisation (Art. 5(1)(c)):

- Collect only the personal data necessary for the outreach purpose
- Do not enrich with data beyond what is needed for the specific campaign
- Document what data you hold and why for each prospect

### ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC) -- Electronic Communications

Governs email, phone, and electronic messaging for marketing.

Key requirements:

- Email marketing to individuals: opt-in required (Art. 13)
- Soft opt-in exception: if email obtained in context of sale/negotiation
  of similar product AND opt-out offered at collection AND in every message
- B2B corporate email (e.g. info@company.com): generally permitted
  without opt-in in most member states
- B2B named individual email: varies by member state (see below)

Member state variations (critical):

- Germany (UWG): very strict; prior consent required for cold email
  to individuals even in B2B context; corporate addresses permitted
- France (CNIL): B2B cold email permitted if relevant to professional
  role; must include opt-out; clear identification of sender required
- Netherlands (ACM): prior consent required for email to individuals;
  B2B corporate emails generally permitted
- Scandinavia: generally follows ePrivacy Directive; B2B legitimate
  interest accepted for initial contact

ESCALATION TRIGGER: Cold email campaign to German individual email
addresses (firstname.lastname@) -> obtain consent or use corporate
addresses only

### Data Protection Officer (DPO) Notification

If your organisation has a DPO:

- Notify DPO of new marketing data processing activities
- Document legitimate interest assessment for outreach campaigns
- Maintain records of processing activities (Art. 30)

### Right to Erasure (Art. 17)

- Prospect can request deletion of all their personal data
- Must comply within 30 days (one calendar month)
- Must delete from CRM, email lists, enrichment databases, and backups
- Document the deletion for compliance records

### Data Subject Access Requests (Art. 15)

- Prospect can request all personal data held about them
- Must respond within 30 days
- Must provide: what data, purpose, source, recipients, retention period

### Cross-Border Data Transfers

- EU prospect data transferred outside EEA requires adequate safeguards
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) or adequacy decision required
- US-based CRM systems: verify EU-US Data Privacy Framework adequacy
  or SCCs in place

ESCALATION TRIGGER: Any CRM or enrichment tool storing EU prospect
data outside the EEA -> verify transfer mechanism with counsel

## KEY EU-SPECIFIC OUTREACH TRIGGERS

- Legitimate interest: document LIA for every B2B outreach campaign
- Germany: do NOT cold email individual addresses without consent;
  use corporate/role-based addresses
- Opt-out: every email must include clear, one-click opt-out mechanism
- Right to object: honour immediately; no "processing your request" delay
- Data minimisation: only enrich with data necessary for the campaign
- DSAR readiness: be prepared to provide all data held on any prospect
  within 30 days
- Erasure: delete all prospect data within 30 days of valid request
- Cross-border: verify transfer mechanisms for non-EEA CRM/tools
- Record keeping: maintain Art. 30 processing records for all campaigns
