## GOVERNING FRAMEWORK

This overlay provides awareness for automated systems generating outreach
content targeting Pakistan-based prospects. This is NOT legal advice. Consult
qualified legal counsel for compliance requirements.

### Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act 2016 (PECA)

Pakistan's primary cybercrime legislation with provisions affecting
commercial electronic communications.

Key provisions relevant to outreach:

- Section 24 (Spam): sending unsolicited electronic communications
  in bulk is an offence
- Definition of spam: messages sent to recipients without prior consent
  or existing business relationship
- Penalties: fine up to PKR 50,000 for first offence; PKR 100,000
  and/or imprisonment for repeat offences
- Applies to email, SMS, and other electronic messaging

Practical guidance for outreach systems:

- Individual targeted B2B outreach (non-bulk, personalised) is
  generally distinguished from prohibited spam
- Bulk unsolicited campaigns to Pakistani recipients require caution
- Always include opt-out mechanism in commercial communications
- Maintain suppression lists for opted-out contacts

### Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (PDPA)

Pakistan's comprehensive data protection law, influenced by GDPR.

Key requirements for outreach:

- Processing personal data requires lawful basis
- Consent or legitimate interest for B2B marketing communications
- Data subjects have right to access, correction, and erasure
- Sensitive personal data: additional consent requirements apply
- Cross-border data transfers: permitted to adequate countries or
  with consent and safeguards

Data enrichment implications:

- Verify that data sources comply with PDPA requirements
- Document the source and lawful basis for each prospect's data
- Data localisation: sensitive personal data may need to be stored
  in Pakistan -- verify with counsel for cloud-based CRM systems

ESCALATION TRIGGER: Bulk outreach campaign to Pakistani prospects
using enriched data -> verify PDPA compliance of data sources and
processing lawful basis

### Pakistan Telecommunication Authority (PTA) Regulations

PTA regulates telecommunications including SMS marketing.

Key requirements:

- Unsolicited commercial SMS requires sender registration with PTA
- Shortcode-based marketing SMS: must register with mobile operators
- DND (Do Not Disturb) registry: PTA maintains a registry;
  recipients can opt out of commercial SMS
- Unregistered bulk SMS senders may be blocked by operators

ESCALATION TRIGGER: Any SMS-based outreach campaign in Pakistan ->
verify PTA registration requirements and DND registry compliance

### WhatsApp Commercial Messaging

WhatsApp is a primary business communication channel in Pakistan.

Guidelines for outreach systems:

- WhatsApp Business API: commercial messaging requires WhatsApp
  Business Platform approval and message templates
- Unsolicited WhatsApp marketing: technically against WhatsApp ToS
  but widely practised in Pakistan; distinguish initial contact from
  ongoing marketing
- Initial B2B contact via WhatsApp: common and generally accepted
  in Pakistani business culture when relevant and personalised
- Bulk WhatsApp blasts: prohibited under WhatsApp ToS; risk account
  suspension
- Always provide clear opt-out mechanism
- Respect Jumma (Friday prayers) timing: avoid sending between
  12:00-14:00 on Fridays

### Industry Norms and Cultural Considerations

Business communication culture:

- Professional WhatsApp messaging is standard and expected
- LinkedIn usage is growing rapidly in urban Pakistan (Karachi,
  Lahore, Islamabad) but penetration is lower than US/EU
- Email is standard for formal B2B communications
- Phone calls: acceptable for warm contacts; cold calling less
  common but not prohibited
- Relationship-based selling: referrals and introductions carry
  significant weight

Timing and scheduling:

- Business hours: generally 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM PKT (Mon-Fri)
  Some organisations work Saturday half-day
- Ramadan: reduced business hours; schedule outreach accordingly;
  sensitivity to timing around Iftar and Sehri
- Eid holidays: avoid outreach during Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha
  (typically 3-5 business days each)
- Friday: Jumma prayers 12:00-14:00; avoid scheduling during this window

Language:

- Business communications typically in English for B2B professional contexts
- Urdu may be appropriate for SME outreach in specific sectors
- Formal tone preferred in initial professional communications

## KEY PAKISTAN-SPECIFIC OUTREACH TRIGGERS

- PECA: bulk unsolicited email is a criminal offence; personalised
  B2B outreach is generally distinguished from spam
- PDPA: verify lawful basis for processing prospect data; honour
  erasure requests
- PTA: SMS marketing requires registration; respect DND registry
- WhatsApp: no bulk blasts; personalised B2B contact is culturally
  acceptable; respect Friday prayer timing
- Ramadan: adjust outreach timing and volume during holy month
- Eid holidays: pause outreach during major holidays
- Referrals: relationship introductions carry significant weight
  in Pakistani business culture
- Data localisation: verify PDPA requirements for cloud-stored
  Pakistani prospect data
